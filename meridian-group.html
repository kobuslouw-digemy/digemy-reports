Why Digemy

Meridian Group’s 6,000-strong merchandiser force is the operational backbone connecting FMCG brands to retail shelf performance — but knowledge decay between training cycles quietly erodes execution quality across every store, every day. When merchandisers misread planograms, miss promotional windows, or file inaccurate call reports, clients see it in their audit scores before Meridian sees it on a dashboard. Digemy’s spaced reinforcement platform keeps every merchandiser current, compliant, and commercially effective — at scale, on their phone, between store visits.

“Six thousand merchandisers all execute at the speed of their last training session. In FMCG, that’s a knowledge decay problem hiding inside your KPI reports.”

🧠 How Digemy Optimises ROI

Digemy keeps planogram, OSA, promotional, and reporting knowledge perpetually current through spaced reinforcement delivered on the merchandiser’s phone between store visits. Top 5 Leakage Areas
Leakage 01
Planogram & OSA Compliance Failures
When merchandisers can’t accurately read and execute planograms across multiple client briefs, shelf compliance drops — and FMCG clients notice on their next audit report. Misidentified out-of-stocks and incorrect facing counts compound into systemic KPI failure across hundreds of stores simultaneously.
Loss Ratio1%–2%
Annual LeakageR13m–R26m
Recovery @10%R1.3m–R2.6m
Effects
Client KPI scores deteriorate, triggering formal reviews and scope reductions. Repeated compliance failures create a reputational risk that accelerates contract churn — often before Meridian’s internal reporting identifies the pattern.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy deploys planogram and OSA micro-modules directly to merchandisers’ phones, reinforced through spaced repetition tied to client brief schedules — so knowledge is current when compliance is measured, not just when training happens.
Leakage 02
NPD & Promotional Launch Execution Gaps
FMCG brands pay a premium for first-to-shelf execution on new product launches and promotional activations — and the first six weeks are critical. Merchandisers who haven’t absorbed the launch brief misplace POS material, sequence distribution incorrectly, and forfeit the early adoption window that determines NPD success.
Loss Ratio20%–35% shortfall
Annual LeakageR13m–R23m
Recovery @10%R1.3m–R2.3m
Effects
Brands absorb the cost of failed launches through lost distribution and early mark-downs, then attribute execution failure to Meridian’s field force quality. This directly threatens renewal of high-value campaign contracts.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy can push a launch brief as a structured learning module to all 6,000 merchandisers within hours of go-live — with comprehension checkpoints before the first store visit, ensuring day-one execution matches the brief every time.
Leakage 03
Promotional Window Execution Failures
Promotional periods are time-bounded — a missed setup window means a lost uplift week that cannot be recovered. Merchandisers who don’t understand promotional hierarchies, price mechanics, or activation deadlines execute late, partially, or incorrectly — turning high-value campaign periods into liability events.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1%
Annual LeakageR6.5m–R13m
Recovery @10%R0.65m–R1.3m
Effects
FMCG clients receive post-campaign reports showing below-target activation compliance, prompting performance reviews and scope renegotiations. Repeat failures erode trust faster than any sales conversation can rebuild it.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy’s campaign-linked modules deploy promotional briefs with built-in deadline awareness — quizzing merchandisers on setup requirements and timing before activation day, not after the window has passed.
Leakage 04
SLA Non-Compliance & Client Churn Exposure
Meridian’s contracts are performance-linked — client SLAs typically include minimum compliance scores, call coverage targets, and data accuracy thresholds. A field force operating below knowledge standard creates systemic SLA exposure that accumulates quietly until a quarterly review forces a renegotiation or a competitor wins the pitch.

Annual LeakageR6.5m–R19.5m
Recovery @10%R0.65m–R1.95m
Effects
SLA breaches trigger formal remedy periods and, in high-value contracts, financial penalties. Repeated underperformance accelerates competitor displacement — field marketing is a relationship business where trust is won slowly and lost quickly.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy’s analytics dashboard gives Meridian’s operations team real-time visibility of knowledge gaps by team, region, and client — turning compliance risk from a lagging indicator into something that can be actioned before the next client review.
Leakage 05
Field Data Accuracy & Reporting Compliance
Meridian’s operational value to clients depends on the quality of data flowing back from the field — but merchandisers who don’t know how to correctly complete call reports, audit records, or raise exception flags produce a corrupted data layer that both management and clients rely on. Inaccurate store-visit data delays corrective action, distorts compliance scores, and erodes client trust in Meridian’s reporting long before a formal audit exposes the gap.

When audit data doesn’t match physical reality, clients begin commissioning independent store checks — an early warning sign of contract review. Once a client stops trusting Meridian’s own compliance reporting, the relationship is in decline regardless of actual execution quality on the ground.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy trains merchandisers on correct reporting protocols, field app usage, and exception-raising procedures through scenario-based micro-modules — ensuring the data flowing into management dashboards and client scorecards actually reflects what’s happening on shelf.
