Why Digemy
AFGRI Group sits at the intersection of food security, agricultural productivity and rural economic development in South Africa — and its commercial performance depends on the quality of knowledge deployed daily by thousands of frontline workers who operate silos, advise farmers on inputs and credit, and service John Deere equipment through the most pressure-intense moments of the agricultural calendar. When a silo operator misapplies a grading standard, an agri retail consultant recommends the wrong crop protection product, or an equipment technician misdiagnoses a fault during harvest, the losses are immediate, cascading and often invisible on any standard P&L. AFGRI doesn’t have a recruitment problem or a product problem — it has a knowledge consistency problem that compounds across 250+ silos, hundreds of retail points and a national equipment dealership network.

Digemy is built for exactly this operating environment. Its spaced repetition microlearning platform closes the gap between what frontline workers are trained to know and what they actually apply under pressure — by embedding knowledge in short, role-specific modules that are reinforced over time, tracked for completion and tied directly to the operational behaviours that protect and grow revenue. The FLM Academy case study demonstrated what this looks like at scale: a 60× increase in engagement, a 154% improvement in knowledge retention and a 70% reduction in ramp time for new hires — outcomes that translate directly into fewer grading errors at AFGRI silos, better input recommendations at agri retail counters and faster competence ramp for seasonal workers joining at peak intake.
“In agri-business, a frontline advisor who gets the recommendation wrong doesn’t just lose a sale — they damage the farmer’s season, and with it, years of trust that took decades to build.”

🧠 How Digemy Optimises ROI

Digemy targets behaviour-driven leakages across agri retail advisory accuracy, silo and grain-handling compliance, equipment diagnostic discipline, financial services product knowledge and new-hire ramp time — using its unique combination of training technology, proven methodologies and real-world frameworks to reduce these leakages FAST, by increasing retention, application and accountability across every frontline role.

Digemy Skills Matrix

The Digemy Skills Matrix for AFGRI would be structured across four primary operational domains: Grain Management & Silo Operations, Agri Retail Advisory, Equipment Services and Financial Services Compliance. Within each domain, Digemy maps the specific knowledge competencies required per role, the current gap severity, the leakage consequence of that gap, and the micro-learning intervention designed to close it — tracked via Digemy’s completion and retention analytics so managers can see exactly which teams are at risk and where the knowledge is not yet sticking.

For AFGRI, the highest-priority matrix entries would centre on silo intake grading accuracy (where errors create direct payment and regulatory consequences), agri retail crop input recommendation confidence (where advice quality is the primary competitive differentiator), and equipment diagnostic sequencing (where technician errors during harvest create cascading losses for both AFGRI and the farming customers it serves). The Skills Matrix becomes AFGRI’s single source of truth on where its frontline knowledge is strong, where it is fragile, and what it costs when it fails.
