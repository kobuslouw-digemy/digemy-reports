Why Digemy
Barloworld Equipment operates at the intersection of technical complexity and commercial scale — the authorised Caterpillar dealer for southern Africa, managing a frontline of field technicians, parts counter staff, rental coordinators and sales representatives whose knowledge and behavioural consistency determines the performance of a ~R20bn revenue engine. The challenge isn’t capability — it’s consistency. A field technician who gets the diagnosis right 80% of the time still generates repeat call-outs, warranty complications and customer trust erosion 20% of the time. A parts counter rep who mis-picks once a week still costs the business more than most training programmes save in a year. At this scale, the margin between excellent frontline execution and average frontline execution is measured in hundreds of millions of rands annually.

Digemy is built for exactly this environment. Its mobile-first, spaced-repetition microlearning platform reaches people where traditional training cannot — on site, between jobs, at the counter — reinforcing the right technical knowledge, SOPs and commercial habits at the moment they’re needed. Where a classroom course is forgotten within days, Digemy’s methodology drives retention of over 154% improvement, measurable engagement 60 times higher than conventional e-learning, and ramp times 70% faster for new hires. For Barloworld Equipment, that translates directly into better first-time fix rates, higher parts accuracy, stronger rental utilisation and fewer warranty rejections — all without adding headcount or taking technicians off-site.
“In earthmoving, the real cost of a knowledge gap isn’t a wrong answer in a quiz — it’s a machine sitting idle on site, a warranty claim rejected, or a R15m customer who never comes back.”

Top 5 Leakage Areas
Leakage 01
Parts Counter Accuracy & Mis-Pick Failure
When parts counter staff misidentify a component, quote the wrong part number or fulfil the wrong item, the cost cascades — machine downtime extends, a re-delivery is needed, customer trust erodes and the margin on the original transaction is consumed by the correction. On high-value Cat parts, a single mis-pick can cost more than an entire day of counter sales.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.0%
Annual LeakageR100m–R200m

Incorrect part deliveries; Extended machine downtime; Re-delivery costs; Warranty claim complications; Customer attrition to competitors; Counter staff confidence erosion
The Digemy Difference
Reinforces parts identification knowledge, SIS navigation habits, supersession awareness and counter verification routines — so the right part leaves the counter the first time, every time.
Leakage 02
Field Service First-Time Fix Failure
A field technician who cannot diagnose correctly on the first visit — or who lacks the parts knowledge to carry the right components to site — triggers a return visit that costs Barloworld Equipment in travel, labour, customer goodwill and machine revenue loss on the client’s side. In mining, every hour of downtime carries a cost the client can quantify exactly — and will.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.5%
Annual LeakageR100m–R300m
Recovery @10%R10m–R30m

Repeat site visits; Escalated repair costs; Customer downtime liability; SLA penalty exposure; Technician credibility loss; Reduced field service margin
The Digemy Difference
Delivers Cat model-specific fault diagnosis micro-learning, pre-job checklists, parts preparation habits and technical reference reinforcement — reducing repeat visits and improving first-time fix rates across the field team.
Leakage 03
Rental Fleet Underutilisation & Poor Extension Conversion
Rental coordinators who don’t proactively manage fleet availability, miss extension conversations with active clients, or fail to communicate idle fleet to the sales team leave revenue sitting in the yard. A Cat machine not earning is a depreciating asset — and every idle day is margin that cannot be recovered.

Leakage 04
Warranty Claim Rejection & Documentation Failure
Warranty claims rejected by Caterpillar due to poor documentation, incorrect failure codes, or missed procedures represent pure revenue loss — costs already incurred by Barloworld Equipment that are never recovered. With the volume of warranty transactions across the SA dealer network, even a modest rejection rate compounds into significant annual write-offs.
