Why Digemy

M-Kopa's business model is built on a simple but fragile promise: put the right device in the hands of the right customer, and the repayments follow. That promise breaks the moment a Direct Sales Agent mis-qualifies a customer, skips the activation walkthrough, or fails to explain the USSD payment process clearly. Digemy's spaced reinforcement platform converts one-time field training into daily knowledge discipline — embedding the qualification rigour, onboarding precision, and collections coaching that M-Kopa's credit model depends on but can't audit at scale.

“If your agent doesn't know how to qualify a customer, your credit model doesn't matter — the loss is already baked in before the device leaves their hands.”

Digemy helps companies like M-Kopa reduce agent-driven credit losses by reinforcing qualification standards, activation coaching, and payment-process scripting through daily microlearning. Top 5 Leakage Areas
Leakage 01
Agent Sales Quality & Customer Mis-qualification
M-Kopa's PAYG model is a credit decision disguised as a sales transaction — every device disbursed carries repayment risk. When DSAs apply inconsistent qualification criteria, losses are systemic and invisible until the defaults surface weeks later in the credit book.
Loss Ratio1.5%–2.5%
Annual LeakageR15m–R25m
Recovery @10%R1.5m–R2.5m
Effects
Mis-qualified accounts inflate the early default rate, erode the unit economics M-Kopa's funding model depends on, and generate collections cost on accounts that should never have been approved. Every ineligible customer is a device lost and a funder relationship strained.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy reinforces affordability assessment frameworks, income-verification decision trees, and red-flag identification daily — turning qualification compliance from a training event into an embedded field habit.
Leakage 02
First 30-Day Activation & Engagement Dropout
A customer who doesn't activate their device and begin using data within the first month rarely recovers — payment motivation collapses when the product isn't embedded in daily life. This window is where agent coaching either pays off or costs the business dearly.
Loss Ratio0.8%–1.5%
Annual LeakageR8m–R15m
Recovery @10%R0.8m–R1.5m
Effects
Low early engagement correlates directly with first-payment default — once a device is locked, recovery costs spike and agent time is consumed by avoidable escalations that erode field productivity.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy embeds step-by-step activation coaching into DSA post-sale behaviour, reinforcing the onboarding walkthrough that converts a new customer into a paying, engaged one within the critical first 30 days.
Leakage 03
Collections Coaching Failure & Missed Payment Guidance
Most M-Kopa customers are first-time credit users — how to pay, when to pay, and how to avoid device lock is knowledge that agents must deliver precisely and consistently. Unclear payment guidance is the leading cause of preventable delinquency in PAYG portfolios.
Loss Ratio1.0%–2.0%
Annual LeakageR10m–R20m
Recovery @10%R1.0m–R2.0m
Effects
Preventable delinquency accumulates fast — a missed first payment more than doubles the probability of eventual default, driving up collections cost and reducing the net portfolio yield M-Kopa reports to debt funders.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy locks in payment-coaching scripts through spaced repetition, ensuring every customer leaves the sale with a clear, consistent understanding of their payment obligations and the USSD process that underpins them.
Leakage 04
Cross-Sell & Product Upsell Conversion Loss
M-Kopa's portfolio extends beyond smartphones to Smart TVs, solar products, and financial services — but DSAs focused on device volume rarely pitch the broader suite. The upgrade conversation is the highest-margin moment in the customer lifecycle, and it's being left on the table.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.0%
Annual LeakageR5m–R10m
Recovery @10%R0.5m–R1.0m
Effects
Missed cross-sell moments mean M-Kopa acquires customers once and monetises them once, when the model is designed for lifetime value accumulation across multiple product cycles and repeat financing relationships.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy reinforces product knowledge and upsell conversation frameworks through bite-sized daily modules, making the cross-sell pitch a natural part of every agent interaction rather than an afterthought.
Leakage 05
Agent Fraud & KYC Documentation Errors
In high-volume field distribution, fraudulent applications — ghost customers, falsified IDs, inflated income claims — are a known risk that scales with agent headcount and sales pressure. Inadequate KYC training creates the gaps that bad actors exploit with predictable regularity.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.0%
Annual LeakageR5m–R10m
Recovery @10%R0.5m–R1.0m
Effects
Fraud losses are hard to recover and harder to detect — by the time a fraudulent account defaults, the device is gone and the agent is often unreachable. Prevention through consistent KYC discipline is the only effective control mechanism at field scale.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy's compliance modules reinforce KYC verification steps and fraud red-flag identification through spaced repetition, ensuring documentation standards are applied consistently across a geographically dispersed field force.
