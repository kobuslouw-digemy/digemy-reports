Top 5 Leakage Areas
Leakage 01
Section 54 Safety Stoppages
When DMRE inspectors find unsafe conditions at a working section, Section 54 of the MHSA empowers them to immediately halt all operations at that shaft — typically for one to five days. At Northam’s production scale, a single shaft-level stoppage can cost R15m–R40m in lost production before the first corrective action is logged. Recurrence risk is highest where team-level compliance knowledge is inconsistent across shifts.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.5%
Annual LeakageR165m–R495m
Recovery @10%R16m–R50m
Effects
When a Section 54 order lands, operations halt across entire shaft sections regardless of where the unsafe condition was found, compounding lost production by the day while management scrambles through corrective submissions and inspector re-evaluations. The downstream signal to institutional investors and the JSE is equally damaging, as stoppage events are SENS-reportable and closely tracked by mining analysts.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy embeds verified MHSA compliance knowledge into the pre-shift routine of every supervisor, safety rep, and artisan across all three mines — with completion and competency data auditable in real time. When DMRE inspectors arrive, every team member can demonstrate current, verified knowledge of the specific compliance areas under review.
Leakage 02
Metallurgical Recovery Underperformance
PGM concentrator circuits are highly sensitive to reagent mix, pulp density, and pH — variables that shift continuously across every shift. When operators lack deep familiarity with circuit response characteristics, PGM recovery drops by one to three percentage points, translating directly to unrecoverable revenue permanently lost in tailings.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.5%
Annual LeakageR90m–R270m
Recovery @10%R9m–R27m
Effects
Each percentage point decline in PGM recovery at Northam’s concentrator throughput volumes equates to tens of millions in lost refined metal value annually. Inconsistent reagent dosing across shifts creates cumulative, invisible revenue erosion that rarely surfaces in post-shift reporting until a quarter-end reconciliation flags the gap.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy delivers plant-specific micro-modules on reagent optimisation and circuit monitoring to concentrator operators across every shift, building the real-time pattern recognition that experienced operators carry in their heads. Newer and rotating operators receive the same verified knowledge baseline — not a diluted handover version of it.
Leakage 03
Trackless Equipment Downtime
Booysendal’s mechanised operation depends on high-availability LHDs, drill rigs, and ancillary fleet — equipment that is expensive to idle and far more expensive to repair after operator error. Knowledge gaps in pre-operational checks and operating limits cause preventable damage and unplanned downtime that cascades across every face in the section.

Annual LeakageR55m–R165m

Effects
A single LHD or drill rig out of service for 24 hours removes a face from the production cycle, forcing section supervisors into reactive replanning that cascades into metre-per-day underperformance across multiple headings. Equipment abuse from inadequate operator competency also accelerates major component failure, inflating maintenance capex unpredictably.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy delivers equipment-specific pre-op inspection and operating procedure modules directly to trackless operators before each shift, creating a verified competency trail that maintenance teams and section managers can monitor in real time. Competency gaps surface before equipment does.
Leakage 04
Shift Handover & Communication Failures
Underground production continuity depends on high-fidelity shift handovers — but across multi-level operations spanning kilometres of workings, critical information about hazardous conditions, pending maintenance, and incomplete tasks routinely degrades between outgoing and incoming teams. The consequences range from repeated stoppages to fatal incidents traced directly to knowledge lost between shifts.
Loss Ratio0.5%–1.5%
Annual LeakageR115m–R345m
Recovery @10%R12m–R34m
Effects
Incomplete handovers cause incoming teams to re-enter sections under the wrong risk assumptions, repeat already-addressed work, or miss developing geological and mechanical hazards. At scale across three mines and multiple shafts, this creates a systemic pattern of production inefficiency and elevated incident risk that handover templates alone cannot resolve.
The Digemy Difference
Digemy supplements handover registers with targeted micro-assessments that confirm incoming team knowledge of current section conditions, pending risks, and recent procedural changes. Every handover moment becomes a verified knowledge checkpoint — not a verbal briefing that fades within an hour underground.

Contractors working in underground sections, concentrator plants, and surface infrastructure represent a disproportionate share of near-miss and incident exposure when their retained knowledge decays within weeks of induction.
