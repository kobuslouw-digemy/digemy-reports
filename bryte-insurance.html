Leakage 01
Claims Leakage & Fraud Detection Gaps
Commercial claims in South Africa carry material fraud exposure — from inflated property losses to staged liability events — and under-trained assessors consistently miss the signals. When handlers lack deep policy wording and exclusion knowledge, incorrect settlements follow, inflating loss ratios and generating errors-and-omissions risk.

Annual LeakageR70m–R140m
Recovery @10%R7m–R14m
Effects
Loss ratios inflate, IBNR (incurred but not reported) reserves are miscalculated, and technical profit erodes quarter by quarter. Fraudulent claims that go unchallenged set precedents that attract repeat exposure across the book.
The Digemy Difference
Spaced reinforcement on fraud indicators, policy exclusion scenarios, and settlement authority protocols — with Digemy’s manager dashboards tracking each handler’s knowledge confidence in real time.
Leakage 02
Underwriting Accuracy & Adverse Selection
Mis-rated commercial risks — through incomplete exposure data, wrong class assignment, or inadequate survey findings — produce under-priced policies that only reveal themselves at claim. Bryte’s multi-class portfolio amplifies this exposure across property, engineering, liability, and motor fleet lines.

Annual LeakageR50m–R100m
Recovery @10%R5m–R10m
Effects
Adverse loss ratios by class erode technical profit and create reinsurance recovery shortfalls on large losses. The portfolio gradually skews toward under-performing risks that are structurally difficult to correct mid-term.
The Digemy Difference
Weekly underwriting scenario drills by class of business, KPI-linked to loss ratio targets — with Digemy’s analytics surfacing knowledge gaps before they become pricing errors on the next renewal.
Leakage 03
First-Year Policy Lapse & New Business Attrition
Commercial policies that lapse in year one deliver acquisition cost with no return — and in a broker-intermediated model, poor onboarding and product understanding at point of sale is a primary driver. Brokers who can’t fully articulate the product’s value struggle to retain clients when renewal pressure hits.

Effects
New business GWP erodes faster than the book grows, customer acquisition cost inflates, and broker relationships weaken through repeated placement-and-lapse cycles. First-year lapse disproportionately damages profitability given upfront commission and underwriting spend.
The Digemy Difference
Structured onboarding reinforcement for both internal account teams and broker-facing channels — ensuring product value is internalised before the first renewal conversation begins.
Leakage 04
Broker Knowledge Gaps & Placement Quality
Bryte’s distribution runs through a large broker network, and the quality of placement decisions depends directly on how well brokers understand the product range. Knowledge gaps produce wrong covers, inadequate limits, and missed specialty placements — increasing E&O exposure and reducing Bryte’s competitive positioning at quote stage.

Incorrect placements generate dispute costs, errors-and-omissions claims, and damaged broker relationships. Specialist products are under-distributed because brokers lack the confidence to recommend them over standard market alternatives.
The Digemy Difference
Broker-targeted product knowledge modules deployed via Digemy — with completion tracking and gap assessments that give Bryte’s broker management team real-time visibility into placement readiness across the network.
Leakage 05
Regulatory Compliance & TCF Failures
FSCA’s Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) framework places direct accountability on insurers for how policies are explained, sold, and administered — and non-compliance stems almost entirely from knowledge gaps in client-facing teams. POPIA obligations compound the regulatory risk for firms handling large volumes of commercial client data.

Compliance microlearning modules with audit-ready completion records — ensuring every client-facing role can demonstrate TCF and POPIA competency to the FSCA, not just awareness.
